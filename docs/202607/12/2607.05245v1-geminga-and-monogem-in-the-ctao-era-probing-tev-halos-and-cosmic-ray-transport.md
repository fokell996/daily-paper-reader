## 摘要
Geminga和Monogem (B0656+14) 周围的TeV晕揭示了中年脉冲星附近扩散受到强烈抑制的区域。确定这些慢扩散区域的大小和磁场强度对于模拟轻子输运以及评估它们对本地正电子过剩的贡献至关重要。切伦科夫望远镜阵列天文台 (CTAO) 将提供扩展伽马射线发射的角分尺度成像。我们使用GALPROP v57为Geminga和Monogem构建两区域扩散模型，根据脉冲星自旋历史注入电子和正电子，并追踪它们通过演化的慢扩散泡和周围星际介质的传播。通过校准HAWC和Fermi-LAT的测量结果，我们通过官方的CTAO仪器响应函数（包括现实的弥散和仪器本底）对模型进行正向折叠。对于50小时的观测，两个晕在任一站点均以高显著性（约13–30σ）被探测到。在乐观的本底假设下，CTAO能够区分高能注入指数Δγ1 ≈ 0.2和磁场强度ΔB ≈ 2 μG的变化。对于Monogem，紧凑的慢扩散泡（RSDZ ≈ 30 pc）可以与扩展的（≥50 pc）情况区分开来，尽管由于有限的视场，非常大的泡仍然简并。在拟合中使用替代的银河弥散模板会使探测显著性和参数灵敏度改变不到10%，这表明对合理的弥散本底模型误差具有鲁棒性。

## Abstract
TeV halos around Geminga and Monogem (B0656+14) reveal regions of strongly suppressed diffusion near middle-aged pulsars. Determining the sizes and magnetic-field strengths of these slow-diffusion zones is important for modelling lepton transport and assessing their contribution to the local positron excess. The Cherenkov Telescope Array Observatory (CTAO) will provide arcminute-scale imaging of extended gamma-ray emission. We construct two-zone diffusion models for Geminga and Monogem with GALPROP v57, injecting electrons and positrons according to the pulsar spin-down history and following their propagation through an evolving slow-diffusion bubble and the ambient interstellar medium. Calibrating to HAWC and Fermi-LAT measurements, we forward-fold the models through official CTAO instrument response functions, including realistic diffuse and instrumental backgrounds. For 50 h observations, both halos are detected with high significance ($\sim 13$--$30,σ$) from either site. Under optimistic background assumptions, CTAO can distinguish changes of $Δγ_1 \simeq 0.2$ in the high-energy injection index and $ΔB \simeq 2,μ{\rm G}$ in magnetic-field strength. For Monogem, compact bubbles with $R_{\rm SDZ}\approx30,{\rm pc}$ can be distinguished from extended ($\gtrsim50,{\rm pc}$) cases, although very large bubbles remain degenerate because of the finite field of view. Using an alternative Galactic diffuse template in the fit changes detection significances and parameter sensitivities by $\lesssim10%$, indicating robustness against plausible diffuse-background mismodelling.
