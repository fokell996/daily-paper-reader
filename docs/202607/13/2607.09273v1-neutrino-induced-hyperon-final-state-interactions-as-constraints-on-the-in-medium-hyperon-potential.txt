(Dated: July 13, 2026) Hyperon single-particle potentials UY (ρ) govern both hyperon propagation in nuclei and the appearance of hyperons in dense matter, where they soften the neutron-star equation of state and lower the maximum mass—the “hyperon puzzle”. > I. INTRODUCTION

The appearance of hyperons in the dense interior of a neutron star relieves the nucleon Fermi pressure and soft-ens the equation of state, lowering the maximum mass below the 2 M⊙ of the heaviest observed pulsars—the 

hyperon puzzle . Whether hyperons appear, and how far they soften the star, is set by their in-medium single-particle potentials UY (ρ), which are only loosely fixed by hypernuclei and Σ-atoms at and below saturation den-sity and are essentially unmeasured above it. A terres-trial handle on UY , independent of the astrophysical and heavy-ion inputs, would therefore be valuable. We show that accelerator (anti)neutrinos provide such a handle. Charged-current interactions on 40 Ar cre-ate Λ and Σ hyperons inside the nucleus—through the Cabibbo-suppressed quasi-elastic reaction ¯ νμN → μ+Y

on antineutrino beams and Cabibbo-favoured associated production on neutrino beams—and the hyperons prop-agate out through nuclear matter, feeling UY (ρ) on the way. The trapped-Λ fraction, the escaping momenta and the Σ → Λ conversion are observables at SBND and DUNE that respond monotonically to UΛ and UΣ—complemented by a final-state-interaction Σ + tag whose momentum tracks UΣ while its yield peaks near UΣ ≈ 0; fed through a hyperonic equation of state they map onto the neutron-star maximum mass. This paper devel-ops that chain end to end, building on the strangeness-production studies of Ref. [1]. The calculations use StrangeMC, an internal multi-channel Monte Carlo of strange-particle production and intranuclear transport on argon—a research tool rather than released software—described in Sec. XIII. Its pro-duction layer is calibrated to the published ∆ S = 0 , 1cross sections [2–5] and cross-checked against the inclu-sive MicroBooNE CC-K+-on-argon measurement [6]; the nuclear initial state and the intranuclear cascade are in-herited, with their existing validation, from the LUNAR proton-decay package [7] (Sec. XIII). A condensed ac-count of the physics result appears in the companion Letter [8]. Because the chain developed below spans a terrestrial measurement, a nuclear transport model and an astro-physical extrapolation, it is worth stating at the out-set which links carry which epistemic weight. Measured 

(in the sense of directly constrained by the projected data) is the in-medium hyperon potential over the sub-saturation densities at which the hyperons are actually born—that is, the function UY (ρ ≲ ρ0), not the single number UY (ρ0). The observables respond to UY at the production density, so the anchor UY (ρ0) and the low-density exponent γ of Eq. (1) enter only through a near-degenerate combination, and quoting a precision on the anchor requires either marginalising over γ or stating the 

γ prior assumed (Sec. X B). Also measured is the hyperon transport at ρ ≲ ρ0. Inferred , through the nuclear model that connects the observables to the potential, are the trapping criterion, the transport prescription by which the potential acts, and the Y N cross sections used in the cascade; these set the systematic floor. The hyperon– nucleon final-state cross section is the leading systematic on UΣ and only a minor one on UΛ, which is why we treat 

UΛ as the robust handle throughout and UΣ as promis-ing but presently systematics-limited. Model-dependent 

is everything above saturation: the supra-saturation con-tinuation ( cY , β ) of Eq. (1), the choice of RMF parametri-sation and baryon content, and therefore the onset den-sity, the maximum mass Mmax and the tidal deformabil-ity. The neutrino data do not constrain the high-density sector; they anchor its low-density input, and the propa-gated stellar numbers quoted in this paper are inferences conditional on a stated EOS framework and an external high-density prior.  

> II. THE NEUTRON-STAR HYPERON PUZZLE

Pulsar timing has established the existence of neutron stars with masses close to or above 2 M⊙ (PSR J1614 −2230 [9], J0348+0432 [10], J0740+6620 [11]). Such masses require a stiff equation of state (EOS) at several times nuclear saturation density 

ρ0 ≃ 0.16 fm −3. At the same time, microscopic calcula-tions of dense matter find that once the baryon density exceeds ∼ 2ρ0 it becomes energetically favourable to convert the most energetic neutrons into hyperons (Λ and Σ −). The new degrees of freedom relieve the Fermi pressure and soften the EOS, generically lowering the maximum mass below the observed value—the hyperon puzzle [12–14]. Whether, and at what density, each hyperon appears is set by its in-medium single-particle potential UY (ρ), and these differ sharply by species: UΛ(ρ0) ≈ − 28 MeV (attractive, fixed by Λ hypernuclei [15]) while UΣ(ρ0) ≈

+30 MeV (repulsive, from Σ − atoms and ( π−, K +) data [16]). A sufficiently repulsive Σ potential removes Σ −

from neutron-star matter [17], and the high-density slope of UY governs how much the remaining hyperons soften the EOS. The strange sector is correspondingly the least-constrained part of the dense-matter EOS, and the three established terrestrial handles—Λ hypernuclear spectroscopy for UΛ, Σ − atoms and ( π−, K +) data for 

UΣ, and heavy-ion Λ directed flow for the high-density slope [18–20] —each carry their own model dependence. Any independent terrestrial constraint directly feeds the astrophysical prediction.

IV. (ANTI)NEUTRINOS AS A PROBE OF THE HYPERON POTENTIAL 

Charged-current (anti)neutrino interactions on nuclei produce hyperons through several channels. The cleanest is the Cabibbo-suppressed (∆ S = 1) quasi-elastic reac-tion on a bound nucleon, ¯νμ + N → μ+ + Y, Y ∈ { Λ, Σ0, Σ−}, (2) which dominates antineutrino beams (∼ 85% of the strange final states for SBND RHC). Neutrino beams add the Cabibbo-favoured (∆ S = 0) associated production 

νN → ℓ Y K and single-kaon production. In all cases the hyperon is born inside the nucleus and must propa-gate out through nuclear matter before detection, feeling the in-medium potential UY (ρ). Four measurable conse-quences follow: 1. Energy/momentum shift. As the hyperon leaves the well its kinetic energy is shifted by UY at the production density; an attractive Λ well lowers the escaping momentum, a repulsive Σ well raises it. 2. Trapping (a hypernucleus-capture proxy). Aslow Λ that cannot climb out of the attractive well is captured (at the transport level; see Sec. IV A); the bound fraction is a direct measure of UΛ.3. Σ → Λ conversion. The strong reaction Σ N →

ΛN in the medium, and the Σ /Λ yield, respond to how the two species are transported. 4. The Σ+ “fake-CCQE” tag. In antineutrino quasi-elastic production the weak vertex obeys ∆S = ∆ Q and can make only Λ , Σ0, Σ−—never 

Σ+. Any Σ + is therefore a pure FSI product (Λ p →

Σ+n, Σ0p → Σ+n), a low-background (kaon-vetoed) tag of in-medium dynamics, potential-sensitive both through the in-medium threshold shift UΣ(ρ) − UΛ(ρ) and through the exit-energy shift UΣ.Because antineutrino beams produce Λ and Σ quasi-elastically while neutrino (FHC) beams reach them through associated production, the ν/ ¯ν pair and the SBND/DUNE energy lever together help separate UΛ

from UΣ; the associated channel at DUNE energies pro-duces copious Σ+, Σ0, a charge state and kinematic regime the antineutrino QE channel cannot reach. Arecent calculation of antineutrino-induced hyperon pro-duction off nuclei [21] already includes a Λ-nucleus FSI potential and Σ → Λ conversion, estimating O(10 4) hy-peron events at SBND, and MicroBooNE has reported the first antineutrino quasi-elastic Λ-on-argon measure-ment [22]. 4

A. The Σ+ fake-CCQE tag 

With the conversion and charge-exchange channels ac-tive, the simulation produces Σ + in antineutrino CCQE events purely through FSI—a final state the weak ver-tex cannot make. The Σ + yield is potential-dependent: it peaks near UΣ ≈ 0 and is reduced both for attractive 

UΣ (exit trapping) and for strongly repulsive UΣ (the in-medium Λ → Σ+ threshold rises with UΣ −UΛ), while the escaping ⟨pΣ+ ⟩ carries the UΣ exit shift (Fig. 7). that controls the Σ − onset in the star. Quantitatively (3 × 10 4 flux-folded antineutrino QE events per beam), a post-FSI Σ + appears in fΣ+ = 1 .0– 1.4% of antineutrino QE-hyperon events, so σ(Σ +) ≃

The inelastic YN parametrisations carry an O(50%) uncertainty. Scaling them by ±50% moves fΣ+ nearly proportionally while leaving the Λ-reco fraction stable to ∼ 1%; propagated through the fit this biases UΣ

by ∼ +162 /−153 MeV (a linearised extrapolation far beyond the grid—in practice the UΣ extraction is lost) while shifting UΛ by only −5/+2 MeV. It is tempting to read this as a pathology of the Σ + “fake-CCQE” rate, which is to leading order a measurement of the YN charge-exchange cross section; we have tested that read-ing and it is wrong. Removing the Σ + observable from the fit entirely leaves the bias at +143 /−151 MeV while loosening δU Σ only from 3 .7 to 3 .9 MeV. The reason is that the Λ momentum spectrum, which carries about two thirds of the UΣ information, is itself shaped by Σ → Λ conversion and hence by the same cross sections. 

The UΣ extraction is Y N -limited whichever observables are used , and a competitive UΣ needs external YN-scattering input rather than a different analysis choice. The trapped-Λ fraction, by contrast, is YN-robust (stable to ∼ 1%), so UΛ remains by far the more secure handle (bias ≲ 5 MeV vs O(150) MeV). (b) Transport prescription (leading on UΛ). The baseline applies the potential as a single exit-energy shift evaluated at the production density [Eq. (1) and Sec. IV A]; the alternative, force-integrated gradient transport follows 

−∇ UY along the trajectory. The two are not equivalent, and the difference is not small: gradient transport raises the trapped fraction at the truth point from 0 .128 to 0.158 (SBND-RHC), 0 .100 to 0 .119 (DUNE-RHC), and correspondingly for the other beams. Fitting exit-shift templates to gradient-transport “data” biases the extracted UΛ by −5.8 MeV (Fisher-weighted over the four beams; −4.7 to −7.1 per beam), because a deeper apparent well is needed to reproduce the enhanced trapping. On UΣ the induced shift is 5–20 MeV with no consistent sign across beams, negligible against (a). This term is comparable to the YN bias on UΛ and roughly twenty times the fixed-γ statistical error, and it is a direct consequence of the transport-level trapping proxy of Sec. > XI. RELATED WORK AND NOVELTY

The two halves of the chain are independently estab-lished, but to our knowledge they have not been joined with neutrinos. On the neutrino side, the elementary and nuclear hyperon-production cross sections have been developed extensively [2, 4, 30–32], and nuclear effects and hyperon FSI implemented in generators [33]; the closest match is the recent calculation of antineutrino-induced hyperon production off nuclei [21], which in-cludes a Λ-nucleus potential and the Σ → Λ conver-sion but stops at cross sections, with no connection to the EOS. On the astrophysical side, the “measure-the-potential → EOS → Mmax ” logic is well developed us-ing other probes—heavy-ion Λ directed flow [18, 19] and chiral-EFT bridging [20]. 

a. Novelty. The specific chain neutrino-induced hy-peron final-state interactions → constrain UΛ, U Σ(ρ) →

hyperonic EOS → TOV → Mmax appears to be new. Its distinguishing feature is that the accelerator-neutrino probe accesses a different production-density and mo-mentum regime and provides a largely independent sta-tistical pull on UY —a complementary fourth leg of the constraint that feeds the neutron-star EOS. The indepen-dence is not complete: its leading systematic, the Y N 

cross section, is shared with the hypernuclear, Σ-atom and heavy-ion extractions. This is the result condensed in the companion Letter [8].

> XIII. METHODS: THE STRANGEMC SIMULATION

All results are obtained with StrangeMC, an internal multi-channel Monte Carlo of strange-particle produc-tion and intranuclear transport on 40 Ar. Each event is built in layers: a bound nucleon is drawn from the nu-clear ground state; a strangeness-production channel and current are selected in proportion to the absolute cross section folded with the beam flux; the produced hyperon feels the in-medium potential UY (ρ) of Sec. III; and every hadron is transported through an intranuclear cascade. The struck nucleon is taken from one of several standard nuclear models (global and local Fermi gas, correlated-tail parametrisations, and the Benhar spectral function) with a removal-energy prescription; the nuclear and cas-cade machinery is adapted from the LUNAR PDK MC package.
