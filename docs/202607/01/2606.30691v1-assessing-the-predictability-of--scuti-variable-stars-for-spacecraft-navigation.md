## 摘要
先前研究表明，船尾座δ型变星可像X射线脉冲星导航一样用于确定航天器位置和时间，但关于光变曲线稳定性以及由此可获得的导航精度仍存在未解决的问题。在此，我们开发了一个计算框架，用于识别光变曲线适合航天器导航目的的船尾座δ型变星。我们的方法强调通过制定度量标准并在航天器导航背景下评估这些度量标准来量化时间不确定性。我们分析了来自开普勒太空望远镜的110多颗船尾座δ型变星以及来自K2任务的额外10颗恒星。对于每颗恒星，我们生成了其归一化流量随时间变化的简单模型，以及用于评估其导航适用性的若干度量标准。通过将预测结果与凌星系外行星巡天卫星（TESS）的观测数据进行比较，进一步评估了模型质量。在本研究调查的120颗船尾座δ型变星中，有32颗被确定为可预测性足够强的候选星，可用于航天器导航。

## Abstract
Previous studies have shown that $δ$ Scuti stars can be used for determining spacecraft position and time similar to X-ray pulsar navigation, but open questions remain regarding the light-curve stability, and, therefore, the navigation accuracy that can be derived from $δ$ Scuti variable stars. Here, we develop a computational framework to identify $δ$ Scuti variable stars with light curves that are suitable for spacecraft navigation purposes. Our approach emphasizes quantifying timing uncertainty through developing metrics and evaluating such metrics in the context of spacecraft navigation. We analyze over 110 $δ$ Scuti variable stars from the Kepler space telescope and 10 additional stars from the K2 mission. For each star, we produce a simple model of its normalized flux as a function of time, along with several metrics used to assess its suitability for navigation. Model quality was further assessed through comparing predictions with observations from the Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS). Out of the 120 $δ$ Scuti variable stars we investigated in this study, 32 stars were identified as candidates predictable enough to enable spacecraft navigation.
