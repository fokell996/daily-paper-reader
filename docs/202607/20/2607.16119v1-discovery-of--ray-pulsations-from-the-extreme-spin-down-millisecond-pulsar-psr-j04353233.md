## 摘要
受最近发现PSR J0435+3233的启发，这是一颗具有异常大的周期导数（$\dot{P}=4.9\times10^{-17}\ {\rm s\,s^{-1}}$）和高自转减慢光度（$\dot{E}=5.89\times10^{37}\ {\rm erg\,s^{-1}}$）的毫秒脉冲星，我们分析了费米大面积望远镜对该脉冲星约17年的观测数据。我们将编目源4FGL J0435.5+3232（距离射电计时位置仅0.01°）识别为PSR J0435+3233的γ射线对应体。利用在射电计时星历有效期内收集的γ射线事件，我们以约6.8σ的置信度探测到其γ射线脉冲。在该区间内，相位分辨分析显示脉冲发射主要集中在旋转相位φ~0.44–0.69内。我们还得出了其γ射线光度$L_γ=6.26\times10^{32}\ {\rm erg\,s^{-1}}$，假设距离为1.2 kpc且辐射各向同性。该光度对应的表观γ射线效率$η_γ\sim1.1\times10^{-5}$，揭示了尽管该脉冲星具有类似于年轻脉冲星的自转能量预算，但γ射线输出异常低。我们的探测将PSR J0435+3233确认为γ射线毫秒脉冲星，其高自转减慢功率与低表观γ射线效率的显著结合，为研究具有极端自转特性的毫秒脉冲星磁层中的粒子加速、辐射束和观测几何提供了新的探针。

## Abstract
Motivated by the recent discovery of PSR~J0435+3233, a millisecond pulsar with an exceptionally large period derivative of $\dot{P}=4.9\times10^{-17}\ {\rm s\,s^{-1}}$ and a high spin-down luminosity of $\dot{E}=5.89\times10^{37}\ {\rm erg\,s^{-1}}$, we analyze $\sim$17~yr of observations obtained with the \textit{Fermi} Large Area Telescope for the pulsar. We identify the cataloged source 4FGL~J0435.5+3232, located only $0.01^{\circ}$ from the radio timing position, as the $γ$-ray counterpart of PSR~J0435+3233. Using $γ$-ray events collected within the validity interval of the radio timing ephemeris, we detect its $γ$-ray pulsations at a $\sim6.8σ$ confidence level. In this interval, the phase-resolved analysis shows that the pulsed emission is concentrated predominantly within the rotational-phase of $φ\sim0.44$--$0.69$. We also derive its $γ$-ray luminosity of $L_γ=6.26\times10^{32}\ {\rm erg\,s^{-1}}$, assuming a distance of 1.2~kpc and isotropic emission. This luminosity corresponds to an apparent $γ$-ray efficiency of $η_γ\sim1.1\times10^{-5}$, revealing an exceptionally low $γ$-ray output despite the pulsar's young-pulsar-like rotational-energy budget. Our detection establishes PSR~J0435+3233 as a $γ$-ray MSP, and the striking combination of its high spin-down power and low apparent $γ$-ray efficiency provides a new probe of particle acceleration, radiation beaming, and viewing geometry in the magnetospheres of millisecond pulsars with extreme rotational properties.
