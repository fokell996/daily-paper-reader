## 摘要
过渡毫秒脉冲星（tMSPs）在射电和X射线脉冲星状态之间交替，可能代表了自转供能与吸积供能中子星之间缺失的环节。其盘状态在低X射线模式和高X射线模式之间随机切换，两种模式的物理起源均未知，且光度低于低质量X射线双星。为了揭示X射线发射的来源，我们进行了吸积盘与tMSP磁层相互作用的二维轴对称广义相对论磁流体动力学模拟。首次利用包含热同步辐射、吸收和康普顿散射过程的辐射传输代码对tMSP模拟进行后处理。通过改变盘密度（即流入速率），我们探索了两种盘状态：一种截断在光柱外侧，另一种截断在光柱内侧。在前者中，大部分X射线通量来自风加热盘驱动的同步辐射：这种“风”模式可能对应于高X射线模式。后者是螺旋桨模式，缺乏这种加热过程。然而，螺旋桨模式会间歇性地驱逐盘，从而激活风加热：这种螺旋桨模式和风模式的70%-30%混合物再现了低X射线模式的X射线谱。螺旋桨模式中过量的电磁力矩使平均两种模式的自转减慢率比无盘的射电脉冲星状态高出几个百分点，与观测一致。总体而言，当流被截断在光柱外侧时，系统在X射线波段更亮，并且支持风致盘加热在低和高X射线模式中都有贡献。

## Abstract
Transitional millisecond pulsars (tMSPs) alternate between radio and X-ray pulsar states, and can represent the missing link between rotation- and accretion-powered neutron stars. Their disk state switches stochastically between the low and high X-ray modes, both of unknown physical origin and less luminous than low-mass X-ray binaries. To reveal the source of the X-ray emission, we carry out 2D axisymmetric general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamical simulations of the interaction between an accretion disk and tMSP magnetosphere. For the first time, we post-process tMSP simulations with a radiative transfer code that incorporates thermal synchrotron, absorption, and Compton scattering processes. By varying the disk density, hence the inflow rate, we explore two disk regimes: one truncated outside and another inside the light cylinder. In the former, most of the X-ray flux comes from the synchrotron emission powered by the wind heating the disk: this "wind" regime could correspond to the high X-ray mode. The latter is the propeller regime and lacks this heating process. However, the propeller episodically expels the disk, activating the wind heating: a 70%-30% mixture of such propeller and wind regimes reproduces the X-ray spectrum of the low X-ray mode. The excess electromagnetic torque in the propeller regime increases the spin-down rate, averaged over both modes, by a few percent above the disk-free radio pulsar state, in agreement with observations. Overall, the system is more luminous in X-rays when the flow is truncated outside the light cylinder and supports a contribution from wind-induced disk heating in both low and high X-ray modes.
