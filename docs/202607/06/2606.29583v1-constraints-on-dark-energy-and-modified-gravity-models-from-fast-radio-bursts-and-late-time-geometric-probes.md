## 摘要
我们研究了104个定位快速射电暴（FRB）与三种成熟的晚期探针——宇宙计时器（CC）、Ia型超新星（SNe）和重子声学振荡（BAO）——相结合时对宇宙学参数估计的影响。通过对三种暗能量模型（ΛCDM、wCDM和CPL）以及三种可行的f(R)引力情景——Appleby-Battye（AB）、Hu-Sawicki（HS）和Starobinsky（ST）模型——进行贝叶斯分析，我们发现FRB显著改善了重子密度Ω_b（改善25%–43%）、哈勃常数H_0（改善12%–35%）和SNe绝对星等M_B（改善10%–32%）的约束。对暗能量参数的约束改善较为温和，在wCDM中w改善约9%，在CPL参数化中(w_0,w_a)改善约(8,22)%。修正引力参数仍然受到较弱约束，仅改善6%–15%，表明当前数据集对偏离ΛCDM的敏感性有限。品质因数分析显示总体改善范围从约48%（ΛCDM）到约91%（CPL），这得益于(H_0, Ω_b)平面精度的提升。模型比较揭示了超越ΛCDM的扩展模型具有中等统计偏好：AIC强烈支持wCDM、CPL、HS和ST（ΔAIC < -7），LRT给出p ≤ 0.004，而BIC返回正值证据（-3.2 < ΔBIC < -2.7）。这些结果表明，FRB可能作为一种互补探针，特别是在约束Ω_b和缓解关键晚期简并性方面具有用处。

## Abstract
We investigate the impact of 104 localized FRBs on cosmological parameter estimation when combined with three established late-time probes: Cosmic Chronometers (CC), Type Ia Supernovae (SNe), and Baryon Acoustic Oscillations (BAO). By performing a Bayesian analysis of three dark energy models ($Λ$CDM, $w$CDM, and CPL) and three viable $f(R)$ gravity scenarios -- the Appleby-Battye (AB), Hu-Sawicki (HS), and Starobinsky (ST) models -- , we find that FRBs substantially improve the constraints on the baryon density $Ω_{\rm b}$ by $25\%$--$43\%$, the Hubble constant $H_0$ by $12\%$--$35\%$, and the SNe absolute magnitude $M_B$ by $10\%$--$32\%$. Constraints on dark energy parameters show more modest improvements, with $w$ improving by $\sim 9\%$ in $w$CDM and $(w_0,w_a)$ improving by $\sim(8,22)\%$ in the CPL parametrization. Modified gravity parameters remain weakly constrained, with improvements of only $6\%$--$15\%$, indicating the limited sensitivity of current datasets to departures from $Λ$CDM. The Figure of Merit analysis shows overall improvements ranging from $\sim 48\%$ ($Λ$CDM) to $\sim 91\%$ (CPL), driven by enhanced precision in the $(H_0, Ω_{\rm b})$ plane. Model comparison reveals moderate statistical preference for extensions beyond $Λ$CDM: AIC strongly favors $w$CDM, CPL, HS, and ST with $Δ\mathrm{AIC} < -7$, and LRT yields $p \leq 0.004$, while BIC returns to positive evidence ($-3.2 < Δ\mathrm{BIC} < -2.7$). These results show that FRBs may be useful as a complementary probe, particularly for constraining $Ω_{\rm b}$ and alleviating key late-time degeneracies.
