## 摘要
中子星（NS）的无量纲潮汐形变度Λ可从引力波（GW）观测中推断，迄今主要用于约束近两倍核饱和密度处致密物质的压力，而核心状态方程在旋进阶段引力波观测中基本不可及。我们基于无量纲恒星结构与潮汐响应方程的微扰分析，采用标度内禀变量，无需依赖具体EOS模型，证明核心EOS可通过Λ直接探测。我们发现了Λ与中心EOS参数X≡P_c/ε_c之间显著的EOS不敏感标度关系，其中P_c和ε_c分别表示中心压力和能量密度。该关系经由大量物理可行的EOS验证。将其应用于GW170817等事件推断的潮汐形变度，可直接确定X。我们进一步推导出沿稳定质量-半径序列的最大质量中子星的紧致下界Λ_TOV≳9.2^{+1.2}_{-1.2}，定量证明了即使最致密的稳定中子星仍明显区分于黑洞（Λ_BH=0）。这些发现揭示了旋进阶段潮汐形变度与核心EOS之间此前未被认识的联系，建立了GW可观测量与强引力区域超致密物质微观物理的直接关联。所得标度将旋进阶段潮汐形变度确立为NS核心EOS的直接且基本不依赖模型的探针。

## Abstract
The dimensionless tidal deformability, $Λ$, of neutron stars (NSs), inferred from gravitational-wave (GW) observations, has thus far been used primarily to constrain the pressure of dense matter near twice nuclear saturation density, leaving the core equation of state (EOS) largely inaccessible to inspiral-phase GW observations. We show that the core EOS can be probed directly through $Λ$ using a perturbative analysis of the dimensionless stellar-structure and tidal-response equations formulated in terms of scaled intrinsic variables, without invoking any specific EOS model. We uncover a remarkable EOS-insensitive scaling relation between $Λ$ and the central EOS parameter $\mathrm{X}\equiv P_{\rm c}/\varepsilon_{\rm c}$, where $P_{\rm c}$ and $\varepsilon_{\rm c}$ denote the central pressure and energy density, respectively. The relation is validated against a broad ensemble of physically viable EOSs. Applying it to tidal deformabilities inferred from events such as GW170817 enables a direct determination of $\mathrm{X}$. We further derive a tight lower bound, $Λ_{\rm{TOV}}\gtrsim 9.2^{+1.2}_{-1.2}$, for maximum-mass NSs along stable mass-radius sequences, quantitatively demonstrating that even the most compact stable NSs remain distinctly separated from black holes, for which $Λ_{\rm{BH}}=0$. These findings reveal a previously unrecognized connection between inspiral-phase tidal deformability and the core EOS, establishing a direct link between GW observables and the microphysics of ultradense matter in the strong-gravity regime. The resulting scaling establishes inspiral-phase tidal deformability as a direct and largely model-insensitive probe of the EOS of NS cores.
