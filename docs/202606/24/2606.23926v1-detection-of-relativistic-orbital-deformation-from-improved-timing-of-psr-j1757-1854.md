## 摘要
PSR~J1757$-$1854是一颗21.5毫秒脉冲星，是一个高度相对论性的双中子星系统，处于紧密偏心（e=0.61）的4.4小时轨道上。它具有极大的引力波光度，并且是已知双中子星系统中轨道衰减最快的系统之一，因此是检验强场区域广义相对论的理想实验室。本文介绍了结合Murriyang望远镜和格林班克望远镜的存档数据，以及MeerKAT射电望远镜的高灵敏度新观测和GBT的额外观测，进行的高精度计时活动的结果。MeerKAT延长的基线时间和卓越灵敏度使得先前测量的后开普勒参数有了显著改进，改进幅度约为2倍或更高。我们报告了在该系统中首次探测到相对论角变形$δ_θ$，使得PSR~J1757$-$1854成为仅有的第三个测量到$δ_θ$的双中子星系统，而这里仅用时9年，而双脉冲星和赫尔斯-泰勒脉冲星都需要数十年的计时。我们展示了如何利用$δ_θ$约束系统的自旋-轨道几何，排除了先前确定的四个几何解中的两个，同时与广义相对论保持一致。我们还评估了对近星点进动$\dotω$的高阶贡献，包括第二后牛顿修正和伦斯-蒂林项，并表明这些项对推断的总系统质量具有可测量的系统效应。在广泛合理的距离范围内，观测到的轨道周期导数$\dot{P}_\mathrm{b}$仍然与引力波阻尼的广义相对论预测一致。

## Abstract
PSR~J1757$-$1854, a 21.5\,ms pulsar, is a highly relativistic double neutron star (DNS) system in a tight eccentric ($e = 0.61$) 4.4\,hr orbit. With extremely large gravitational wave luminosity and one of the fastest orbital decay rates of any known DNS system, it is ideal for testing general relativity (GR) in the strong-field regime. Here we present results from a high-precision timing campaign combining archival data from the Murriyang telescope and Green Bank Telescope (GBT) with new high-sensitivity observations from the MeerKAT radio telescope and additional observations from the GBT. The extended baseline and superior sensitivity of MeerKAT have yielded substantial improvements to previously measured post-Keplerian parameters by a factor of around $\sim2$ or more. We report the first detection of the relativistic angular deformation, $δ_θ$ in this system, making PSR~J1757$-$1854 only the third DNS system for which $δ_θ$ has been measured, achieved here in just 9 yrs compared to the decades of timing required for both the double pulsar and the Hulse-Taylor binary. We demonstrate how $δ_θ$ can be used to constrain the spin-orbit geometry of the system, ruling out two of the four geometric solutions previously identified, while remaining consistent with GR. We also evaluate higher-order contributions to the periastron advance $\dotω$, including the second post-Newtonian correction and the Lense-Thirring term, and show that these have a measurable systematic effect on the inferred total system mass. The observed orbital period derivative, $\dot{P}_\mathrm{b}$ remains consistent with the GR prediction for gravitational-wave damping across a wide range of plausible distances.
