## 摘要
我们提出了一个新的全天模型，用于描述在同步辐射、自由-自由辐射和旋转尘埃主导的低频弥漫银河辐射。该模型通过整合最近的射电和微波巡天数据，扩展了普朗克2015年弥漫成分分离分析。我们拟合了35幅分辨率为1度的全天和部分天空图，包括2.30 GHz的S-PASS、4.76 GHz的C-BASS、10-20 GHz的QUIJOTE，以及来自Cosmoglobe合作项目重新处理的WMAP和普朗克LFI数据，以及普朗克HFI通道。使用贝叶斯参数方法Commander，我们推导出总强度中主导低频前景成分的空间变化幅度和光谱参数图。主要产品包括全天的同步辐射振幅和光谱指数解、具有单分量对数正态光谱模型的旋转尘埃发射的全天表征，以及重建的4.76 GHz全天总强度图，该图追踪弥漫同步辐射且系统误差相对于Haslam 408 MHz有所降低。修正后的低频锚定增加了恢复的同步辐射振幅：在4.76 GHz，它大约是普朗克2015年预测的两倍。该模型在高达353 GHz的天空中95%的区域实现了低于10 μK的均方根温度残差，在银河平面内分数残差低于1.5%，在QUIJOTE波段内低于5%。残差角功率谱比CMB谱低两个数量级以上。这些产品描述了射电和微波发射之间的过渡，并为前景建模和天空模拟应用提供了新的参考。

## Abstract
We present a new all-sky model of low-frequency diffuse Galactic emission in the regime where synchrotron, free-free, and spinning dust dominate. The model extends the Planck 2015 diffuse component-separation analysis by incorporating recent radio and microwave surveys. We fit 35 full- and partial-sky maps at 1 degree resolution, including S-PASS at 2.30 GHz, C-BASS at 4.76 GHz, and QUIJOTE at 10-20 GHz, together with reprocessed WMAP and Planck LFI data from the Cosmoglobe collaboration and Planck HFI channels. Using a Bayesian parametric approach with Commander, we derive spatially varying amplitude and spectral parameter maps for the dominant low-frequency foreground components in total intensity. The main products are a full-sky synchrotron amplitude and spectral-index solution, an all-sky characterization of spinning dust emission with a single-component log-normal spectral model, and a reconstructed all-sky total-intensity map at 4.76 GHz tracing diffuse synchrotron emission with reduced systematics relative to Haslam 408 MHz. The revised low-frequency anchoring increases the recovered synchrotron amplitude: at 4.76 GHz, it is approximately a factor of two higher than the Planck 2015 prediction. The model achieves RMS temperature residuals below 10 $\mathrmμ$K over 95% of the sky up to 353 GHz, with fractional residuals below 1.5% in the Galactic plane and below 5% across QUIJOTE bands. Residual angular power spectra lie more than two orders of magnitude below the CMB spectrum. These products describe the transition between radio and microwave emission and provide a new reference for foreground modeling and sky-simulation applications.
