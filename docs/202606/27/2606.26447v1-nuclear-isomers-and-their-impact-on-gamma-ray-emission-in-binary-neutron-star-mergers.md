## 摘要
GW170817的多信使观测提供了强有力的证据，表明双中子星并合是通过快中子俘获过程产生重元素的场所。不稳定的r-过程核素的衰变会产生大量γ射线，这些γ射线不仅为相关的千新星提供能量，还可能被当前和未来的γ射线观测站探测到，从而直接探测合成的核素。当前的γ射线发射模型将核反应网络与单个衰变光谱联系起来。通常，反应网络只追踪核素的基态布居，在计算衰变光谱时假设子核快速退激。对于存在寿命较长、高能核同核异能素的核素，这种假设可能不准确。在本工作中，我们放宽了这些假设，针对一组选定的核素，对其核同核异能素的动态行为进行建模。这些同核异能素作为独立状态包含在网络中，具有随温度变化的基态与同核异能态之间的有效跃迁率，以及利用核能级结构计算的β-馈送概率。我们估算了来自同核异能素跃迁的γ射线通量，发现Nb-97m的743.3 keV谱线和Y-91m的555.6 keV谱线可能在银河系距离上的γ射线观测站（如COSI、AMEGO和LOX）中可探测到。这些结果是针对含有同核异能素的一小部分核素计算得到的，突显了在核反应网络中稳健纳入核同核异能素的必要性，以及对由此产生的γ射线光谱进行仔细建模以表征观测信号的需求。

## Abstract
The multi-messenger observations of GW170817 have provided strong evidence that binary neutron star mergers are a site of heavy-element production through the rapid neutron capture process. The decay of unstable $r$-process nuclei produces a significant number of $γ$-rays, which not only power the associated kilonova, but may also be observable in current and future $γ$-ray observatories, providing a direct probe of the nuclei synthesized. Current models of the emitted $γ$-rays link a nuclear reaction network with individual decay spectra. Typically, only the population of the ground state of the nuclei is tracked in the reaction network, and rapid de-excitation of the daughter nuclei is assumed when calculating the decay spectra. This may not be accurate in nuclei where there are longer-lived, high-energy nuclear isomers. In this work, we relax these assumptions, modeling the dynamic behavior of nuclear isomers for a select list of nuclei. These isomers are included as independent states in the network, with temperature-dependent effective transition rates between ground and isomeric states and $β$-feeding probabilities calculated using the nuclear level structure. We estimate the $γ$-ray flux from isomeric transitions, finding that the 743.3 keV line of Nb-97m and the 555.6 keV line of Y-91m may be detectable in $γ$-ray observatories such as COSI, AMEGO, and LOX at galactic distances. These results, calculated for a small fraction of nuclei with isomers, highlight the need for the robust inclusion of nuclear isomers in nuclear reaction networks, as well as careful modeling of the resultant $γ$-ray spectroscopy to characterize observational signals.
